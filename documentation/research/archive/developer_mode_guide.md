# ALM v12.0: Developer Mode Guide
## Interpreting the "Under the Hood" Components

The **Developer Mode** output of the ALM (Auditory Language Model) pipeline is generated by the `SituationIntelligenceRenderer` (SIR). Unlike the standard Human Report, which is designed to be easily read by end-users, Developer Mode exposes the exact internal state of the Cognitive State Management Layer. 

It shows you *exactly* what the system perceived, what it rejected, and mathematically *why* it made its decisions.

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to interpret, understand, and analyze each section of the Developer Mode output.

---

### 1. SCENE UNDERSTANDING

This section represents the final, synthesized conclusion of the ALM pipeline after all audio, semantic, and temporal data has been processed.

*   **What is happening:** The final `current_situation` selected by the Hypothesis Reasoning Engine (HRE) as the winning hypothesis.
*   **Human-Oriented Summary:** The deep empathetic paragraph detailing the context, generated natively by the optimized 3B local model.
*   **Where is it happening:** The `environment` determined by the model based on reverberation and contextual clues.
*   **Human Goals (Intent):** The inferred goals of the actors (e.g., escaping, communicating).
*   **Missing Information:** Crucial data points that the model actively knows it is missing (e.g., "visual confirmation", "cause of the explosion").

**How to Analyze:** Look here to see if the model's fundamental grasp of the situation and the human goals are accurate. If this is wrong, the entire downstream projection will be wrong.

---

### 2. EVIDENCE (OBSERVED VS INFERRED)

The system does not treat all data equally. This section lists every piece of evidence the Cognitive State holds and classifies it.

*   **[OBSERVED EVIDENCE]:** Data that was directly detected by a neural network (e.g., Audio Spectrogram mapping to "Glass Breaking", Whisper detecting the word "Help").
*   **[INFERRED EVIDENCE]:** Data that was not physically heard, but logically deduced by the LLM (e.g., if glass breaks and people are screaming, the model *infers* a "Threat or Danger").
*   **Weight:** A float value (e.g., `Weight: 0.85`). Higher weights mean the system trusts this evidence more. High-decibel impacts get higher weights than quiet background hums.

**How to Analyze:** If the model makes a crazy assumption, check this list. Did it hallucinate [OBSERVED EVIDENCE] that wasn't actually there? Or did it place too much weight on an [INFERRED] assumption? 

---

### 3. COMPETING HYPOTHESES

This is the most critical section for developers. The ALM generates multiple explanations for what is happening, and pits them against each other in a mathematical arena.

For each hypothesis, you will see four metrics:
1.  **Semantic Confidence:** How much the LLM *believes* this situation makes logical sense (0.0 - 1.0).
2.  **Acoustic Score:** How well this hypothesis physically matches the raw audio events detected by the audio classifiers (0.0 - 1.0).
3.  **Temporal Score:** How well this hypothesis aligns with the *timing* of events.
4.  **Composite Rank:** The final, weighted average of all scores. The hypothesis with the highest Composite Rank wins and becomes the "Current Situation".

*   **Why it won / Why it lost:** A short justification explaining why the HRE mathematically selected or rejected this hypothesis.

**How to Analyze:** This section helps you debug "close calls." If the model thought an airstrike was just "friendly construction work," look at the Acoustic Score vs Semantic Confidence. Usually, if it fails, it's because the LLM had high Semantic Confidence for a bad idea, but the Acoustic Score (the raw physical audio) was too low to override it. 

---

### 4. REASONING CHAIN

This is the step-by-step internal log (Chain of Thought) from the Transparent Reasoning Engine (TRE). 

It tracks the exact logical steps the system took:
> 1. Detected overlapping human voices and high-energy impact.
> 2. Fused observations: Impact occurred during speech.
> 3. Discarded "Casual conversation" hypothesis due to high-energy impact.

**How to Analyze:** If the model's final conclusion is wrong, read this chain line-by-line. You will find the exact moment the model's logic deviated from reality.

---

### 5. CONFIDENCE & UNCERTAINTY

A numerical representation of the model's self-awareness.

*   **Overall Confidence:** A normalized score combining perception, context, and projection confidence. If this drops below 0.40, the system is essentially guessing.
*   **Uncertainty/Unknowns:** A measure of how much critical data is missing. High uncertainty means the audio is too chaotic, muffled, or ambiguous to make a definitive ruling.

**How to Analyze:** In production environments, if Overall Confidence is low and Uncertainty is high, the system should ideally alert a human operator rather than acting autonomously on the data.

---

### 6. PROJECTION

Generated by the Situation Projection Engine (SPE). It takes the current situation and predicts what is most likely to happen next based on physics, human behavior, and the acoustic trajectory.

**How to Analyze:** If the projection seems completely disconnected from the current situation, it means the SPE rules engine is lacking context for the specific event type that is occurring.
